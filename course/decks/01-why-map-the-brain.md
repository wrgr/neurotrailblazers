# Deck Spec: 01 Why Map the Brain

## Session
- Duration: 70 + 15 min
- Outcome: one testable connectomics hypothesis brief
- Figure source: `course/units/figures/01-why-map-the-brain-selected-v1.md`

## Slide plan
1. Title and track positioning
   - Content: technical track purpose and scope boundaries.
   - Speaker note: frame structure as constraint engine, not full explanation.
2. Why now?
   - Content: scale of modern datasets and tooling maturity.
   - Figure hook: motivation panel from shortlist.
3. Structure and function: valid claims
   - Content: what structure can infer vs cannot infer.
4. Hypothesis translation workflow
   - Content: biological question -> measurable structural endpoint.
5. Measurement units
   - Content: synapse counts, motifs, path lengths, targeting ratios.
6. Evidence thresholds
   - Content: completeness and QC requirements by claim type.
7. Null models
   - Content: why degree-preserving and spatial controls matter.
8. Worked example A
   - Content: recurrent motif hypothesis and falsification criterion.
9. Worked example B
   - Content: cell-type targeting bias hypothesis.
10. Failure modes
    - Content: claim inflation, metric mismatch, dataset mismatch.
11. Practical checklist
    - Content: five-step pre-analysis gate.
12. Activity + bridge
    - Content: write study brief; bridge to data scales.

## Assessment
- Prompt: submit 1-page hypothesis brief with one null model and one explicit limit.
- Rubric: testability, metric fit, uncertainty discipline.
